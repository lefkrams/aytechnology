1. Introduction

AY Technology ("AY Technology", "we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy, confidentiality, and security of the personal information we collect, use, disclose, and store.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, retain, and protect your personal information when you:

  • Visit our website at https://aytechnology.net
  • Contact us for enquiries
  • Engage our services
  • Apply for employment or contractor opportunities
  • Interact with us as a client, supplier, contractor, or business partner

AY Technology is committed to complying with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). Where applicable, we also comply with the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations.

8. Philippine Data Privacy Compliance

Where AY Technology collects, processes, stores, or transfers personal information relating to individuals located in the Philippines, or where our contractors, employees, or business operations involve the Philippines, we are committed to complying with the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations.

In accordance with Philippine privacy laws, we:

  • Collect personal information only for legitimate and lawful purposes.
  • Process information fairly and transparently.
  • Collect only information necessary for our business purposes.
  • Protect personal information using reasonable organisational, physical, and technical safeguards.
  • Retain information only as long as necessary or as required by law.
  • Require our employees, contractors, and service providers to maintain confidentiality and comply with privacy obligations.

Where personal information is transferred internationally, we implement appropriate contractual and technical safeguards to protect the information and comply with applicable Australian and Philippine privacy requirements.

Where applicable under Philippine law, individuals may have the right to:

  • Be informed about the processing of their personal information.
  • Access their personal information.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Object to certain processing activities.
  • Request deletion or blocking of personal information where permitted by law.
  • Lodge a complaint with the Philippine National Privacy Commission.
